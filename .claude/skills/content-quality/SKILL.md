# Content Quality Rules

## 1. The Only Standard That Matters
- Content earns its place by genuinely helping the reader accomplish something, learn something, or decide something
- Google's quality systems do not penalize AI-assisted content — they penalize low-quality content that lacks depth, originality, and real value
- Human content is 8x more likely to rank #1 than unedited AI-generated content — the gap is in specificity, experience signals, and original insight
- The question to ask before publishing any piece of content: would a real person find this genuinely useful, or would they leave immediately and search again?

## 2. Banned Writing Patterns
- Never open with a definition of the obvious: "التسويق الرقمي هو..." or "في هذا المقال سنتحدث عن..."
- Never use transition fillers: "من المهم أن نلاحظ...", "تجدر الإشارة إلى...", "في الختام نستنتج..."
- Never write bullet lists where every item is a single vague line with no explanation
- Never restate the same point multiple times in different words to increase word count
- Never write paragraphs that could apply to any product in any industry without changing a word
- Never produce content where removing any paragraph would not be noticed by the reader
- Never write in a tone that sounds like it is performing helpfulness rather than being helpful

## 3. What Genuine Content Looks Like
- It opens with the reader's actual problem, question, or goal — not with who the company is
- It gives specific answers with real numbers, real examples, real tools, real outcomes
- It demonstrates that the writer has actually done the thing they are describing
- It contains information the reader could not easily find in the first result of a Google search
- It uses Arabic that sounds like it was written by a knowledgeable person who respects the reader
- It acknowledges tradeoffs, exceptions, and nuances — content that claims everything is simple and perfect sounds fabricated
- It flows as a coherent argument or narrative, not as a checklist of loosely connected facts

## 4. E-E-A-T Signals Must Be Present
- **Experience**: Content shows the author has personally encountered the problem — use real scenarios, real outcomes, real observations
- **Expertise**: Content demonstrates domain knowledge — correct terminology, accurate distinctions, knowing what to leave out
- **Authoritativeness**: Pages link to credible sources, cite real data, and reference named experts where relevant
- **Trustworthiness**: Author name and bio are present on all editorial content, publication date and last-updated date are visible, privacy policy and contact information are accessible from the page
- Google uses human quality raters to evaluate E-E-A-T — content that fails this evaluation loses ranking regardless of technical SEO

## 5. Google's SynthID and AI Detection Reality
- Google revealed SynthID at Google I/O 2025 — a watermarking system that embeds invisible markers in AI-generated content
- These markers survive editing and paraphrasing — they are not removed by light human revision
- The correct approach is not to hide AI use but to ensure AI-generated drafts are substantively improved by human editorial judgment
- AI drafts must be supplemented with: real data from the specific market, original observations, regional examples, and opinions that reflect genuine expertise
- Content that reads like AI — vague, over-structured, pattern-repetitive — performs poorly in search regardless of origin, because it fails user engagement metrics

## 6. Content Depth by Page Type
- Blog posts and guides: minimum 800 words, with each section answering a real sub-question
- Product/service pages: minimum 500 words of specific benefit and differentiation content
- Category pages: minimum 200 words of contextual framing text, not just a grid of items
- Landing pages: minimum 400 words of substantial content explaining the offer, the audience, and the value
- FAQ pages: each answer must be at least 3–5 sentences — one-line answers are thin content
- Pages under 300 words of real content pull down the domain's quality signal and risk de-indexing in Google's spam updates

## 7. Arabic Writing Quality Standards
- Use Modern Standard Arabic (MSA) for professional and formal content — it is credible across all Arabic-speaking markets
- Match the register to the audience: formal for B2B and professional services, warm and conversational for consumer products
- Sentences should be direct and clear — Arabic formal writing often becomes unnecessarily complex; fight this tendency
- Avoid mixing Arabic and English in the same sentence — it signals either laziness or poor Arabic language confidence
- Every technical term used in Arabic must be the term that Arabic-speaking professionals actually use in that field — not a literal translation from English
- Spell-check Arabic content meticulously — AI-generated Arabic contains subtle grammatical and diacritical errors that immediately undermine credibility with native readers
- Cultural references, idioms, and examples must be rooted in the target market — a Saudi reader and a Moroccan reader have different reference points

## 8. Blog Post Structure That Ranks
- The first 100 words must contain a clear answer to the main question the post addresses — AI Overviews and featured snippets extract the opening paragraph
- Each section heading must be a complete thought or question, not a topic label — "كيف تحسّن ترتيب موقعك في نتائج البحث؟" is better than "تحسين الترتيب"
- Include at least one concrete example per major section — abstract principles without examples are forgettable
- Include at least one original data point, original observation, or real case from the Arabic market per post — this is what separates your content from the ten others covering the same topic
- End with a specific, actionable next step — not "in conclusion" but "the first thing you should do today is..."

## 9. Trust Signals on Every Page
- Author name and a two-line bio on all editorial content
- Publication date prominently displayed — Arabic readers check dates to judge whether advice is current
- Last-updated date on content that gets revised — showing it was recently reviewed signals active maintenance
- Sources and references for any statistics, percentages, or claims — unsourced statistics are untrustworthy
- Real business information in the footer or About page: company name, country, contact method
- SSL certificate is non-negotiable — HTTP pages signal untrustworthiness immediately to Arabic users who have been exposed to phishing

## 10. Content Maintenance Cycle
- Content older than 12 months that references statistics, software versions, or regulatory information must be reviewed
- Outdated information is not neutral — it is actively harmful to credibility and ranking
- Pages with declining traffic (visible in Search Console) must be diagnosed within 30 days: is the content stale, is it outcompeted, or has the search intent for the keyword changed?
- Never let a page sit at low traffic for months without either improving it or redirecting it — thin, low-traffic pages drag the domain quality score down for all other pages
- Broken outbound links on content pages reduce credibility — audit them quarterly
